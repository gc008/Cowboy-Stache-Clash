COWBOY 'STACHE CLASH

by Garrett Castro

A Cowboy from the West and a Cowboy from the East,

Both with mustaches thoroughly greased,

The Cowboy from the West always professed

That his mustache was better than the rest.

The Cowboy from the East hopped off his great beast,

And stated his mustache was blessed by a priest.

The Cowboy on the left who came from the West

Stood across from the Cowboy from the East,

Whose mustache was just creased

Said, “Walk forward 3 paces,

So we can see each other’s faces.”

The Cowboy on the right was always polite,

But now ready to fight.

He then made a quick bow, then said with a vow,

“The next move is up to you now.”

The Cowboy from the West accepted the request

Then took out his gun, and aimed it at the other man’s chest.

They both stood perspiring,

And then started firing,

Each one both hit,

Slowly began to sit,

Then held their own gashes,

And admired each other’s 'staches.

They both felt they won,

Then stared toward the sun.

Their duel was now done.
